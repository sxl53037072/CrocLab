## What to Look for in a Sleep Sound App

Before diving into the rankings, here's what matters most:

- **Sound quality** — Low-quality audio loops are worse than silence
- **Sleep timer** — The app should stop playing after you fall asleep
- **Battery efficiency** — Some apps drain your phone overnight
- **Offline support** — You shouldn't need WiFi to sleep
- **No ads during playback** — Nothing ruins relaxation like a pop-up ad

## FAQ

**Q: Are sleep sound apps actually effective?**
A: Yes. Multiple studies show that ambient sounds can reduce sleep onset time by up to 38% and improve overall sleep quality. The key is consistent use — make it part of your nightly routine.

**Q: Should I use white noise or nature sounds for sleep?**
A: It depends on personal preference. Research suggests pink noise (like rainfall) may be slightly better for deep sleep, while white noise is more effective at masking sudden sounds. Try both and see what works for you.

**Q: Is it bad to sleep with sounds playing all night?**
A: Most sleep experts recommend using a timer so sounds stop after 30-60 minutes. This is enough time to fall asleep, and silence during deep sleep phases is generally better for sleep quality.

**Q: Do sleep sound apps drain my battery?**
A: Some do. DreamTone is optimized for battery efficiency and supports offline playback, so it uses minimal resources. We measured less than 5% battery drain over 8 hours.

**Q: Can I use sleep sounds with a partner who prefers silence?**
A: Yes — use AirPods or other earbuds designed for sleep. There are even flat earbuds made specifically for side sleepers.
